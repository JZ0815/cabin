A Thrilling Adventure with a Peaceful Stay at Cosby

The Smoky Mountains, with their mist-shrouded peaks and lush green valleys, offer a myriad of recreational opportunities. Among these, river rafting stands out as an exhilarating way to explore the natural beauty of this region. If you're planning a trip here, a stay at Cosby provides the perfect peaceful backdrop after a day of thrilling water adventures.

White Water Rafting in the Smoky Mountains

There are multiple rivers and streams in the Smoky Mountains that offer varied rafting experiences:

1. Pigeon River: Upper Pigeon River: This is a favorite for thrill-seekers and offers Class III and IV rapids, making it suitable for intermediate to advanced rafters. Lower Pigeon River: Ideal for families and beginners, this section of the river offers Class I and II rapids.

2. Nantahala River: Located to the south of the Smoky Mountains, it offers a mix of calm waters and exciting Class II and III rapids, making it suitable for most rafters.

3. Ocoee River: Famous for being the site of the 1996 Olympic white-water events, it offers challenging Class III and IV rapids.

4. French Broad River: This is one of the oldest rivers in the world and offers a combination of calm sections and moderate rapids.

Cosby: The Serene Retreat

Cosby is a smaller and less-crowded alternative to the bustling Gatlinburg and Pigeon Forge, making it a top choice for those looking for tranquility after a day of adventure.

Reasons to Stay at Cosby:

1. Natural Beauty: Cosby is often referred to as the "Hidden Gem of the Smokies." You can enjoy picturesque views, nature trails, and the soothing sounds of Cosby Creek.

2. Proximity to Adventure: Despite its quiet ambiance, Cosby is close to various rafting launch points and outfitters.

3. Local Attractions: The Cosby Campground, a part of the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, is perfect for campers. Plus, the Hen Wallow Falls is a short hike away.

4. Authentic Experience: Experience the local culture with farm-to-table eateries, craft stores, and small-town festivals.
